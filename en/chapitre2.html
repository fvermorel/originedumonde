Cronus’s Rebellion
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Gaia’s resentment against the tyrant who made her suffer was growing. Her last born, Cronus, (Time), was wittier and more capable than the other Titans. The Cyclops, his elder brothers, forged a sickle* (we use a sickle to cut plants) with which Gaia armed Cronus. She asked him to deliver her from Uranus’s violence and allow her to let her children out.
I will be the boss soon...
The Time God’s Sickle
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Cronus cut the tie that united the Earth and the Sky. A dreadful scream then filled the universe: for the first time blood was shed! Uranus flew far away and became the heavenly vault. He would never come close to Gaia again. His place was now on top of the universe. At last there was space for creatures to be born and to grow.
The blood drops will engender lots of monsters you will meet in our coming books: the Erinyes who will punish criminals, or the Lernaean Hydra which will be destroyed by Heracles in the course of his twelve Labors...
Aphrodite’s Birth from the Waves
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Blood drops were falling on the earth, and each of them engendered a terrifying monster which would cause lots of misfortunes later. However, the piece of flesh cut by Cronus had another fate: it fell into the sea and got mixed with sea-foam. There was an astonishing bubbling from which emerged a big shell. While the shell was floating to the shore, it opened. Out of it came the most beautiful creature the universe would ever know: Aphrodite, the goddess of love, without whom our earth would have been very sad.
Although she was the goddess of love, Aphrodite was very dangerous. You will meet her again in our coming book: Love and Psyche.
The Construction of the World
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
The Titans who were enclosed in Gaia’s womb were finally liberated; among them were Pontus, the ocean, and Urea, the big mountains. Our universe was taking shape. But at that time it was not yet very welcoming: the continents were moving in all directions and the Titans were pacing all over the earth. Fortunately things were destined to change...
According to the Greeks, gods and titans are everywhere: for instance, the sun, the moon, and dawn are also titans that are offsprings of Uranus and Gaia.
I am Ocean ! I am Mountain !I am the boss !
